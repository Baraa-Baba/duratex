**MDF vs MDP Explanation:**

- **MDP (Medium Density Particleboard):** Made from wood particles in 3 layers. Excellent resistance to warping. Ideal for straight, load-bearing pieces like shelving.
- **MDF (Medium Density Fiberboard):** Made from wood fibers. Provides smooth, consistent surface. Ideal for detailed cuts, furniture faces, doors, and decorative pieces.
- **HDF (High Density Fiberboard):** Higher density variant used for specialized applications like laminate flooring underlayment.
- **MDF Fire:** Fire retardant variant, compliant with safety standards for commercial projects.
- **MDF Ultra Premium:** Moisture-resistant, ideal for kitchens and bathrooms.

- **2021** — Corporate rebrand from Duratex S.A. to **Dexco S.A.**; ticker changed to DXCO3

## 🖌 Design Guidelines

- **Color Palette:**
  - Primary: Deep warm brown / walnut (`#3d2b1a`)
  - Secondary: Warm off-white / cream (`#f5f0ea`)
  - Accent: Muted gold (`#b5915a`)
  - Text: Near-black (`#1a1a1a`)
  - Light background: Soft warm gray (`#faf8f5`)
- **Typography:** Clean sans-serif (e.g., Inter or Nunito Sans) for body; a slightly stylized serif or display font for headlines
- **Aesthetic:** Warm, natural, premium. Think interior design magazine — clean layouts, large imagery, generous whitespace
- **Image style:** Full-bleed product and environment photography; panels shown in real interior settings
- **Navigation:** Sticky top nav with logo left, links center/right; hamburger menu on mobile
- **Footer:** Dark background (walnut tone), logo, nav links, social icons, sister brand logos, copyright
